About Sleep Coach

Our Mission

Helping you build better sleep habits

Sleep Coach is designed to reduce bedtime friction with simple wind-down plans and soothing soundscapes. We show your sleep patterns in a friendly, non-judgmental way, keeping the interface accessible, responsive, and easy to enhance later.

This is a static HTML + CSS prototype (v0.1) that demonstrates the core functionality and user experience. All analytics, timers, sensors, and audio logic are placeholders that can be wired up later with JavaScript.

How Sleep Coach Works

The Science Behind Our Suggestions

Sleep Coach analyzes your sleep patterns using established sleep science principles:

  • Circadian Rhythm: Your body's natural 24-hour sleep-wake cycle
  • Sleep Debt: Accumulated sleep loss that affects performance and health
  • Sleep Architecture: The structure and quality of your sleep stages
  • Chronotype: Your natural preference for sleep and wake times
  • Sleep Hygiene: Behaviors and environment that promote good sleep

Note: In this prototype, all analysis is based on sample data. Real implementations would use actual sleep tracking and machine learning algorithms.

Common Sleep Myths Debunked

‚ùå "You can catch up on sleep during the weekend"

Fact: While you can partially recover from sleep debt, irregular sleep schedules disrupt your circadian rhythm and make it harder to fall asleep on Sunday night.

‚ùå "Alcohol helps you sleep better"

Fact: Alcohol may help you fall asleep faster, but it significantly reduces sleep quality and REM sleep, leading to less restorative rest.

‚ùå "Watching TV in bed helps you relax"

Fact: The blue light from screens suppresses melatonin production, and engaging content can actually stimulate your brain instead of relaxing it.

‚ùå "Everyone needs exactly 8 hours of sleep"

Fact: Sleep needs vary by individual and age. Most adults need 7-9 hours, but some may function well on 6 hours while others need 10.

When to Seek Professional Help

While Sleep Coach can help with general sleep improvement, certain conditions require medical attention:

  • Chronic insomnia lasting more than 3 months
  • Loud snoring, gasping, or breathing pauses during sleep
  • Excessive daytime sleepiness despite adequate sleep
  • Unusual behaviors during sleep (sleepwalking, night terrors)
  • Persistent difficulty falling or staying asleep
  • Sleep problems affecting work, relationships, or daily functioning

Consult with a healthcare provider or sleep specialist if you experience any of these symptoms.
