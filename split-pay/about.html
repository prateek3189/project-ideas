About Split & Pay

Making bill splitting simple and fair

What is Split & Pay?

Split & Pay is a group expense tracker designed to make splitting bills simple and fair for friends, roommates, and teams. Whether you're planning a trip, managing household expenses, or tracking project costs, Split & Pay helps you keep track of who owes what and provides clear settlement plans.

  • Percentage-based splits
  • Itemized splits for detailed tracking
  • Custom weight-based splits

Split Types Explained

Equal Split

Everyone pays the same amount. Perfect for shared meals, group activities, or when consumption is roughly equal.

Example: ‚Çπ1,200 taxi fare split equally among 4 people = ‚Çπ300 each

Shares Split

Use whole numbers to represent consumption levels. Great for situations where some people consume more than others.

Example: ‚Çπ1,860 lunch with shares 1-2-1-0 = ‚Çπ465, ‚Çπ930, ‚Çπ465, ‚Çπ0

Percentage Split

Split by exact percentages. Useful when you have specific ratios or when shares don't work well.

Example: ‚Çπ3,600 water sports split 25%-25%-25%-25% = ‚Çπ900 each

Itemized Split

Assign individual items to specific people. Perfect for shopping trips or when people buy different things.

Example: Grocery bill with individual items assigned to each person

Weights Split

Use decimal weights for precise splitting. Ideal for complex scenarios or when percentages are too rigid.

Example: ‚Çπ9,800 resort booking with weights 0.3-0.3-0.2-0.2

Settlement Algorithm

Split & Pay uses a minimal cash flow algorithm to determine the most efficient way to settle all balances. The goal is to minimize the number of transfers needed while ensuring everyone gets paid what they're owed.

How It Works

  1. Calculate Net Balances: Determine who owes money and who is owed money
  2. Find Optimal Transfers: Identify the minimum number of payments needed
  3. Handle Rounding: Adjust the last payment to account for rounding differences
  4. Provide Alternatives: Offer different settlement strategies
Fairness Note: The algorithm ensures that rounding differences are handled fairly, typically by adjusting the last payment in the sequence.
