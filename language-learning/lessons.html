Grammar Fundamentals

Grammar

Present Tense -AR Verbs

Learn the basic conjugation pattern for regular -AR verbs in Spanish.
View Examples & Patterns

Conjugation Pattern:

  • Yo: -o (hablo, trabajo)
  • Tú: -as (hablas, trabajas)
  • Él/Ella: -a (habla, trabaja)
  • Nosotros: -amos (hablamos, trabajamos)
  • Vosotros: -áis (habláis, trabajáis)
  • Ellos: -an (hablan, trabajan)

Practice:

Conjugate "estudiar" (to study) in all persons.

Grammar

Gustar - Expressing Likes

Master the unique structure of "gustar" and similar verbs in Spanish.
View Examples & Patterns

Structure:

Me gusta(n) + noun/infinitive

  • Me gusta el café (I like coffee)
  • Me gustan los libros (I like books)
  • Me gusta estudiar (I like studying)

Other Forms:

Te gusta, le gusta, nos gusta, os gusta, les gusta

Cultural Context & Notes

Cultural

Greetings & Social Customs

Understand Spanish-speaking cultural norms for greetings and social interactions.
View Cultural Insights

Greeting Customs:

  • Kissing on both cheeks is common in many countries
  • Handshakes are appropriate in formal situations
  • Use "usted" with older people or in formal settings
  • Always greet people when entering a room

Common Greetings:

  • ¡Hola! - Hello!
  • Buenos días - Good morning
  • Buenas tardes - Good afternoon
  • Buenas noches - Good evening/night
Cultural

Dining Etiquette

Learn about dining customs and etiquette in Spanish-speaking cultures.
View Cultural Insights

Dining Customs:

  • Wait for the host to begin eating
  • Keep your hands visible on the table
  • It's polite to try everything offered
  • Compliment the food - "¡Está delicioso!"

Useful Phrases:

  • ¡Buen provecho! - Enjoy your meal!
  • ¿Me pasa la sal? - Can you pass me the salt?
  • La cuenta, por favor - The bill, please
Cultural

Business Culture

Understand business etiquette and communication styles in Spanish-speaking countries.
View Cultural Insights

Business Etiquette:

  • Personal relationships are important
  • Meetings may start late - "hora española"
  • Use formal language initially
  • Dress conservatively for meetings

Communication Style:

  • Indirect communication is common
  • Build rapport before business
  • Respect hierarchy and titles
  • Follow up with written confirmation
Cultural

Regional Variations

Explore how Spanish varies across different countries and regions.
View Cultural Insights

Vocabulary Differences:

  • Spain: "coche" vs Latin America: "carro"
  • Spain: "ordenador" vs Latin America: "computadora"
  • Spain: "vosotros" vs Latin America: "ustedes"

Pronunciation Variations:

  • Spain: "th" sound for "c" and "z"
  • Argentina: "sh" sound for "ll" and "y"
  • Caribbean: "r" becomes "l" in some words

Tú vs Usted

Master the distinction between informal and formal address in Spanish.
View Usage Patterns

Use "Tú" (Informal):

  • With friends and family
  • With children and teenagers
  • In casual social situations
  • With colleagues you know well

Use "Usted" (Formal):

  • With older people
  • In business settings
  • With strangers
  • In official situations
