Problem #12: Linear Equation

We'll solve this by breaking it into small steps. Try each hint in order before revealing the solution.

Problem Statement

Solve for x: 3x + 5 = 20

Given: 3x + 5 = 20
Unknown: x
Goal: Find the value of x

Guided Plan

  1. 1 Isolate the variable term
  2. 2 Subtract 5 from both sides
  3. 3 Divide both sides by 3

Step 1: Isolate the Variable Term

We need to get the term with x by itself on one side of the equation.

üí° Hint 1: Undo addition first

Since we're adding 5 to 3x, we need to subtract 5 to undo this operation.

üí°üí° Hint 2: Subtract 5 from both sides

Remember: whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other side to keep the equation balanced.

3x + 5 - 5 = 20 - 5

üéØ Final Hint: The result

After subtracting 5 from both sides: 3x = 15

Common Mistake: Trying to divide by 3 first instead of subtracting 5.

Remember the order of operations - addition/subtraction comes before multiplication/division when solving equations.

Step 2: Subtract 5 from Both Sides

Now we have 3x = 15. The next step is to isolate x by dividing.

üí° Hint 1: What operation isolates x?

Since x is multiplied by 3, we need to divide by 3 to get x by itself.

üí°üí° Hint 2: Divide both sides by 3

This gives us: x = 5

Common Mistake: Forgetting to divide both sides equally.

If you only divide the left side by 3, the equation becomes unbalanced.

Step 3: Divide Both Sides by 3

Final step to get x = 5 and verify the solution.

üí° Hint 1: Perform the division

3x √∑ 3 = x and 15 √∑ 3 = 5

üí°üí° Hint 2: Verify the solution

Checks for Understanding

Explanation: False. You should subtract 5 first to isolate the variable term, then divide by 3.
