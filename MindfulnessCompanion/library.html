Meditation Scripts

Body Scan Script

10 min Relaxation

A complete body scan meditation script for deep relaxation and body awareness.

View Full Script
Introduction

Find a comfortable position, either sitting or lying down. Close your eyes gently and take three deep breaths, feeling your body settle into the surface beneath you.

Starting with the Feet

Bring your attention to your feet. Notice any sensations - warmth, coolness, pressure, or perhaps no particular sensation at all. Simply observe without trying to change anything.

Moving Up the Legs

Slowly move your attention up through your legs - calves, knees, thighs. At each area, pause briefly to notice what's present.

Hips and Lower Back

Continue upward to your hips and lower back. These areas often hold tension. See if you can allow any tightness to soften.

Abdomen and Chest

Move to your abdomen and chest. Notice the gentle rise and fall of your breath. Feel the rhythm of your breathing.

Shoulders and Arms

Bring awareness to your shoulders and arms. Many people carry tension here. See if you can release any unnecessary holding.

Neck and Head

Finally, move to your neck and head. Notice your jaw, your forehead, the area around your eyes. Allow any tension to dissolve.

Complete Awareness

Now bring your attention to your entire body as a whole. Feel the sense of completeness and wholeness.

Closing

Take three more deep breaths, and when you're ready, gently open your eyes.

Loving-Kindness Script

7 min Compassion

A heart-opening practice to cultivate compassion for yourself and others.

View Full Script
Setting Intention

Begin by finding a comfortable seated position. Place your hands gently on your heart or lap. Take a few deep breaths to center yourself.

For Yourself

Start by directing loving-kindness toward yourself. Silently repeat: "May I be happy. May I be healthy. May I be at peace. May I be free from suffering."

For Someone You Love

Now bring to mind someone you care about deeply. Picture them clearly and repeat: "May you be happy. May you be healthy. May you be at peace. May you be free from suffering."

For a Neutral Person

Think of someone you neither like nor dislike - perhaps a neighbor or colleague. Wish them well: "May you be happy. May you be healthy. May you be at peace. May you be free from suffering."

For Someone Difficult

Now bring to mind someone you find challenging. This is the heart of the practice. Wish them: "May you be happy. May you be healthy. May you be at peace. May you be free from suffering."

For All Beings

Finally, extend this wish to all beings everywhere: "May all beings be happy. May all beings be healthy. May all beings be at peace. May all beings be free from suffering."

Closing

Take a moment to feel the warmth of these wishes in your heart before gently opening your eyes.

Focus & Concentration

5 min Focus

A short practice to sharpen concentration and improve mental clarity.

View Full Script
Preparation

Sit comfortably with your back straight but not rigid. Rest your hands gently in your lap. Close your eyes or soften your gaze.

Breath Focus

Bring your attention to your breath. Notice the natural rhythm of inhaling and exhaling. Don't try to change it, just observe.

Counting Breaths

Begin counting your breaths. Inhale, count "one." Exhale, count "two." Continue up to ten, then start over.

When Mind Wanders

When your mind wanders (and it will), gently return to counting. Don't judge yourself - this is normal. Each return is a moment of mindfulness.

Deepening Focus

As you continue, try to maintain focus on the counting. Feel the rhythm becoming more steady and your mind becoming more clear.

Closing

When you're ready to finish, take three deep breaths and gently open your eyes.

Sleep Preparation

12 min Sleep

A gentle wind-down practice to prepare your mind and body for restful sleep.

View Full Script
Getting Comfortable

Lie down in your bed in your usual sleeping position. Make sure you're comfortable and won't be disturbed.

Body Relaxation

Starting from your toes, consciously relax each part of your body. Feel the tension melting away as you move upward.

Breath Awareness

Focus on your breath, making it slower and deeper. Imagine each breath carrying away the day's stresses.

Mental Wind-down

Let go of any thoughts about tomorrow or today. Imagine your mind as a lake, with thoughts as ripples that gradually settle.

Visualization

Picture yourself in a peaceful place - perhaps a garden or by the ocean. Feel the calm and safety of this space.

Drifting Off

Allow yourself to drift naturally into sleep. Don't worry about staying awake - just let go.
